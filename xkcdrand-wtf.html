It's pretty simple really. If you click on xkcd's "Random" button, it will redirect you to a "random" comic.

If you just reload the XKCD Random Number Generator or hit "Go" without entering a maximum and minimum value, you'll get the number of the comic which XKCD randomly decided to give you that time.

If you provide a minimum and maximum value, we'll use XKCD's random number to give you a corresponding number in your range.

  • So let's say I want to flip a two sided coin but I want XKCD's random number generator to be the arbiter of whether I landed on heads or tails. I just put 1 for minimum, 2 for maximum, check the "Integers Only" box, and hit go.
  • Or, if you have a room with 23 puppies and are having a hard time deciding which is the cutest, assign each one a unique number, 1 through 23, set those in minimum and maximum (and also set "Integers Only") and hit go. Bam! That one is the cutest.
  • The percentage of the people in the world who believe this is actually useful? Set minimum to 0, maximum to 10,0 and hit Go.

Quick explanation of the math:

Ok so how do we convert a random XKCD number to a number in your range?

First, let's set the xkcd range. That's the range from the first comic to the current comic. In our example, let's go with 1 to 920.
920 - 1 = 919
Let's say the random number we get back from xkcd is 430.
430 is 46.74...% of the way from 0 to 919.
Ok, so your range is -20 to 342.12?
Well, the absolute value of your maximum minus your minimum is 362.12.
46.74...% of the way from 0 to 362.12 is about 169.25.
Now let's add you original minimum to account for our initial adjustment:
169.25 + (-20) = 149.25
So the random number in your range is 149.25.
Or 149 if you said integers only. We round to the nearest whole number.
